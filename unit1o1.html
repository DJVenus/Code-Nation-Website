  • 1.1 - Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

    • Information technology (IT) is an integral part of every single business plan [1]. It can assist us in a variety of applications in business from improving communication with stakeholders to improving production efficiencies and can even help with budgetary implications by improving cost efficiencies [2]. IT is so crucial to success in business today that research over the last two decades has shown that companies who choose to embrace and invest in technology, with innovation at the forefront of their decision making processes, increase their market share, financial figures and overall competitiveness [3]. In summary I would suggest that the purpose of IT in modern business is to improve every facet of business and I believe we are now at a stage where modern business and the economy would crumble without IT.

  • 1.2 - Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – What do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

    • Collaboration is crucial to the success of all work-based projects but it is particularly crucial when it comes to digital projects. Without a variety of methods, skills and resources; collaboration would be very difficult. The current pandemic has only increased reliance on these solutions. Trello is a particularly effective tool for collaboration that incorporates the kanban model in a web based platform that contains cards that you can assign tasks to into sections such as to do, doing, review and done. This enables digital projects to be organised and tasks to be delegated live in real-time which solves any geographical constraints as long as you have a computer and access to the internet. Slack is another great resources for collaboration. It allows users to share resources, send messages, share code, images and it even allows user to call each other. Again it is a live web-based platform so has all the connectivity benefits of Trello.

  • 1.4 - Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – What causes digital projects to fail? Why?

    • Somewhere between 70% and 90% of digital projects fail [4]. There are a variety of reasons for these failures from poor strategy to unrealistic goals but external risks are rapidly becoming the biggest problem when using IT solutions. Cybercrime is a common risk to digital projects; particularly when working on important projects or projects that involve sensitive data. Internet privacy is of paramount importance when working digitally and all team members need to stay vigilant towards these risks when working digitally. This is why workplace cyber security is vital in modern businesses with phishing in particular is a hugely popular technique designed to take advantage of low levels of user security awareness, accounting for a third of all data breaches in 2019 [5].

  • 1.7 - Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

    • Along with budget, time and quality of projects, legal guidelines are the most difficult constraints teams face when working on digital projects [6]. Since it's introduction in May 2018, General Data Protection Regulation or GDPR gives everyone control of their personal data and information. This is particularly important to companies working on digital projects as fines for not adhering to GDPR are anywhere up to 4% of the companies gross profit or €20 million [7]. This means it is of paramount importance that companies and employees comply with legal guidelines and constraints and it has a huge impact on digital projects.
