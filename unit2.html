  • 1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

    • When creating websites the constraints fall into two main categories: physical and psychological [8]. Physical constraints are anything that affects the how the site works such as paths within the website that help you navigate through the website whereas psychological constraints as features such as symbols and mapping which also affect usability but are not physical in nature and more comes down to individual preferences when it comes to the interface. This is why it is crucial to ensure that use design flexibly to cater for multiple user who may have different hardware for example such as size of screen [9]. There are also legal constraints such as copyright that need to be taken into account. This means developers must be careful to ensure they have permission to use any content they add to their websites such as images, text and audio [10]. Failure to adhere to these legal constraints can lead to fines and even more serious implications for both the business and the individual.

  • 1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

    • One of the main issues when using the internet is access for the blind. According to statistics in the US; two-thirds of blind individuals' transactions over the internet are abandoned due to their inability to access parts of a website [11]. There are however some assistive technologies that enable blind users to improve accessibility. These include screen readers and dictators that convert the text on the website into audio and vice-versa as well as braille displays that convert the text into braille using a unique braille keyboard [12]. Even thouogh these technologies are in place it seems from my research that accessability for the blind is still a major issue and certainly something I will consider and take into consideration going forward.
